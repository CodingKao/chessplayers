Chess rating is a measure of a player's skill level. It is used to evaluate a player's performance in a game and to compare them with other players. The most widely used rating system is the Elo rating system, which was developed by Arpad Elo in 1960. The Elo rating system is based on the idea that a player's rating should be proportional to their performance. It assigns each player a rating based on their performance in a game, as well as the strength of their opponents. The higher the rating, the better the player. The Elo rating system is used in many chess tournaments and is the official rating system of the World Chess Federation (FIDE).
